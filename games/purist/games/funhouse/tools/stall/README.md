# Funfair round stall — reproducible build

`assets/meshes/props/funfair_stall.glb` is generated by one deterministic script. It carries its OWN
materials and embedded textures; the manifest must NOT set `material`/`tint` on it (that override is
for skinning untextured meshes and would repaint the whole stall).

```bash
BL="/c/Program Files/Blender Foundation/Blender 4.4/blender.exe"
"$BL" --background --python build_stall.py -- ../../assets/meshes/props/funfair_stall.glb
```

Fixed RNG seed → same bytes every run. No in-place mutation: the script never reads the GLB it writes.

## What it builds
A travelling hoopla **round stall**, from researched construction:

- **10 convex shutters** (6" bow on the chord). Not a hexagon — hexagons are modern event-hire stock and
  absent from the historic record; 10 (sometimes 8) is what the sources show. The shutter ring IS the
  structure; the legs are light.
- **Counter at 0.85m** — the manifest's `pos.y: -85` lands the carrom board exactly on it. In a real
  hoopla the prizes sit on a central table, which is precisely where the board is.
- **Tilt and pelmet are one material** — the same proofed cloth, stripes running continuously from
  canopy to scalloped hem. Proofed canvas is waterproofed and reads with a sheen; an exposed WEAVE
  reads as sacking, so there is no fabric scan here by design.
- **Counter UVs run along the shelf's longest side** (the chord), with v scaled by the real
  depth/length ratio so the grain follows the run instead of stretching ~5:1 across it.
- **Lamps at 80mm centres, 2700K**, on the INSIDE face of the eaves beam ring capping the pillars,
  plus runs up each rafter. Nothing sits below that beam. The lamp is a real CC0 bulb, instanced —
  360 nodes referencing ONE mesh. It should be a cabochon (a domed jewel through a board), but no
  CC0 cabochon exists anywhere; see `art/SOURCES.md`. The rafter lamps are INFERRED — the record is silent on stall interiors — but the player
  sits under this roof and an unlit tilt reads as a dark tent.
- **Ageing comes from the real scans**, not procedural noise. fbm chalk, rain streaks, ring-strata
  chips and alligatoring all read as painted-on dirt and fought the real ornament — they are gone.
  The scan's own scratches, scuffs and AO carry the wear. Still procedural: varnish amber, and the
  canopy's stripes + storage creases.
- Fairground paint is a translucent glaze over metal leaf → modelled as coloured METAL. That is what
  throws the lamplight back, and it is the whole look.

## Sources — all inside this repo (the engine is never a build dependency of content)
| Surface | Source |
|---|---|
| Panel paint | `rough` set, tinted, tiled `PANEL_TILE`x under the ornament |
| Counter + timber | `Carrom-Engine/assets/textures/rough` — neutral, so it tints to any livery |
| Tilt + pelmet | proofed canvas, procedural — stripes + folds over the paint scan's drift |

The `rough` set is the one build-time path outside this repo. It is shared engine `assets/`
(which ship with the engine, not in content packs), and it is what `table.glb` skins its wood
with — so the stall's timber matches the table the board sits on.

**Canopy UVs are PER BAY, not per canopy.** 1024px across the whole 18.3m canopy is 17.9mm per
texel — a stripe *edge* blurs over ~2cm. Per-bay gives 1.79mm. The seam lands on the rafter,
which is where a real canvas tilt is seamed. 4 bands/bay = 40 around, and W,R,W,R still
alternates across the seam.

**The counter is not flamboyant.** Leaf-under-glaze is the ornamented panels' look; a working
top is plain trade paint (metallic 0, matte). Left metallic it reads as coloured plastic.
Its UVs tile, so its noise is PERIODIC and its knocks are scattered + wrapped — edge-weighted
chips and strap bands are panel ideas that would repeat into a pattern.
| Panel ornament | `art/racinet_pl9_scroll.png` — Racinet 1877, **public domain** |
| Lamp mesh | `art/polyhaven_lightbulb_01.blend` — Poly Haven, **CC0** |

Both are documented in `art/SOURCES.md`, including what was rejected and why.

The loose `assets/meshes/**/*.jpg|png` beside the GLBs are gitignored Godot Extract-Textures artifacts —
never build from them, they will not survive a fresh clone. Read the embedded images out of the GLB.

## Preview
```bash
"$BL" -b --python preview_stall.py -- <glb> <out.png> [hero|seat|game|both]
```
`game` renders the REAL camera envelope (`CameraOrbit`/`CameraConfig`: Fov 55 **vertical**, orbit
25–105cm, elevation 10 → −80 where sub-10 becomes head-pitch-up). Blender fits the sensor to the larger
dimension by default, which silently narrows the vertical FOV on a landscape render — `sensor_fit` is
pinned VERTICAL so the preview matches the game rather than judging through a tighter lens.

Framing, measured: at default zoom the top of frame sits 7.5° BELOW horizontal, so the lights can never
appear there at any stall size — you see the shutters. They land in shot at max zoom-out, and the roof
is the payoff when the player leans back.

## Lighting the ring — three separate mechanisms, easy to confuse

1. **Emissive bulb glass** — makes each lamp *glow*. Strength 2.4: the engine's `GlowHdrThreshold` is
   1.6, and anything much higher clips to white BEFORE it can bloom, which reads as a flat blob.
2. **Emissive strip** (`BulbStrip`) behind each bank — a real marquee run bleeds into one line of
   light rather than separate dots. Blooms and fills the gaps between bulbs.
3. **`BayLamp0..9`** — actual point lights, ONE PER BAY. Emission in Godot illuminates *nothing*; it
   is a bright surface, not a lamp. Without these the stall is lit only by the scene rig and the
   bulbs look stuck on. One per bay, not one per bulb: 310 point lights is not affordable, and at
   this radius a single warm source per bay is indistinguishable from its 13 bulbs.

`export_lights=True` is required or the lamps never leave Blender. The engine must also KEEP them:
`ScenePropsBuilder.ConfigurePropLights` used to `QueueFree()` every OmniLight3D a prop carried.

## Baked ambient occlusion
The engine's `SsaoRadius` is **1.1cm** — tightened so the seam between touching coins reads on the
board. The counter overhangs its wall by ~280mm, ~25x that radius, so screen-space AO can never
produce that shadow and widening it per flavour would wreck the coin AO in the same scene. So it is
baked, into the glTF OCCLUSION slot (ambient only, never burned into albedo).

It needs a SECOND UV set: the shutters are `bowed_panel` + `solidify`, so inner and outer faces share
UV0 — bake there and the unoccluded outer face overwrites the occluded inner one, and the shadow
vanishes into a flat white map. `UVAO` is packed non-overlapping per object, so bays can still share
materials. Lamps are excluded: 310 share one mesh, and instancing cannot have per-object atlas regions.

Cost: ~+16MB. Blender merges the AO into each material's ORM as one PNG per material. It is correct
(occlusion samples at UV1, metallic-roughness at UV0 — each channel in its own space) but unavoidable.

## The scan sources are NOT part of this model

`build_stall.py` imports `coin_black.glb` and `table.glb` for ONE reason: to read the embedded PBR
scans out of them. Their meshes are discarded immediately (`read_factory_settings`) — the pixels are
already copied into numpy by then.

**This stall builds a stall and nothing else.** The table and coins come from the engine's own board
scene; they have no business inside a prop. If that discard is ever lost the sources export INSIDE the
prop — which happened, silently, costing 25MB and 14k faces. There is now an assert before export that
fails the build if any non-stall mesh is present. Do not remove it.

## Banners, motifs, ground

- **Banners, not shutters.** Flat panels between the pillars in the sign board's livery (maroon + gold
  rule), made of the SAME proofed canvas as the roof — a banner is cloth, so no wood grain, no moulded
  bead, and the gold lining is printed rather than raised (a banner has no arris to drown in paint).
- **Every 2nd banner carries a motif**, alternating a crown and PREMIUM/CLUB. The crown replicates the
  engine's own house emblem (`EmblemMeshes.GetHouseEmblem` — three peaks topped with balls over a solid
  trapezoid); the proportions are lifted from it so the stall wears the same crown as the coins.
- **Ground**: churned earth, not grass. The research is blunt that a fair operates in a quagmire, that
  mud washes off and is NOT cumulative, and that what is permanent is bare trodden dirt. World-scaled
  UVs so the scan repeats instead of stretching across 6m.
  The engine's own floor sits at −116cm (`table_height` 1100mm) and the stall's ground at −84.6cm, so
  the stall's earth is what shows. If `table_height` ever drops below ~845mm the engine floor will
  rise through it.

## Gold rules must never be sub-pixel
Thin lining SHIMMERS at grazing angles. Mipmaps do not fix it — they average a sub-pixel line into the
flicker (and `mipmaps/generate=true` is already on). Rules are >=7px at TEX with a wide soft ramp so
they survive minification. If lining ever shimmers again, widen it; do not reach for the filter.
